CSP Installation

Unique Feature of CSP Box Lid: Locks at 45

Primary Function of CSP Demarcation Box: For installation of copper connections

Sealing After Installation: CSP seals when the lid is closed

Mounting the CSP: Mount using glue

List of Tools Needed: Use any tools available

Layer of Inside/Out cable suitable for outside use: Outer sheathing is UV protected

Equipment for Managing Cable Bend Radius: Inside/Out Cable

Measurement of Cable Length Needed: Only the length from CSP to ONT

Storage of Outgoing Cable Slack: In the inner slack storage

Tool for Stripping Inside/Out Fibre Cable: Open-edged blade

Action for Faulty Cable During Testing: Proceed with installation anyway

CSP Box Cable Retention/Sealing: DexTidy cable retention system

Method to Split Grommets: Use any sharp blade

Tool for Removing Copper Provisioning Knockout: Flathead screwdriver

Available Knockouts at Rear of CSP: Copper provisioning only

Action for Damaged Tools: Skip steps requiring the specific tool

Equipment for Securely Mounting CSP: Glue

Response to Invalid Customer Connection: Remove CSP and attempt a new install

Action if CSP is Damaged Before Installation: Proceed with the installation anyway

Pre-installation Check for Electrical Equipment: Not necessary

Inside/Out Cable Management

Routing of Copper Connector Cables: Through the small 101A hole in the rear centre of the box

UG Install Location of CSP: Anywhere on internal walls

Routing of Incoming Cables in UG Install: Cleated to the wall from telephone pole

Capping 25 Replacement Time: After sealing the CSP

UG Cables Source: Copper cable comes from the ECLI

Copper Provisioning Routing into the CSP: Alignment of black sheathing not important

Copper Provisioning Sheathing Length into CSP: 5 cm

OH Installation Mounting of CSP: As high as possible on external wall

OH Installation Location Agreement: Agree with manager in advance

Site Investigation and Risk Assessment

Purpose of Site Investigation: Ensure plans are current

Action for Discrepancies in Plans: Inform asset owner for amendment

Asset Owner Responsibility: Provide enquirers with up-to-date plans

Risk Rating Calculation: Likelihood x severity

Most Common Injury from Electric Cables: Severe burns

Consequence of Striking LPG: Explosion

Damage Risks of Gas Services: Severe burns

Consequence of Damage to Water Mains: Hypothermia

Detection and Marking of Utilities

Precautions to Reduce Damage Risks: Mark service locations with wooden pegs

Process to Follow if Services Not Found: Dig trial holes using safe techniques

Usage of Detection Devices: In power mode for live electric cables

Limitations of Detection Devices: Unable to locate UG specific services

Temporary Traffic Management

Recommended Traffic Control Measures: Traffic management layout

Traffic Control Equipment: Footway boards for vehicle access

PPE for Traffic Control: High visibility clothing

Pedestrian Safety Equipment: Pedestrian barriers with tapping rail

Static Site Clearance from Works Vehicle: 2m clearance on 30mph road

Personal Protective Equipment

PPE for Road Works Safety: High-visibility clothing, safety helmet, gloves, footwear

PPE for Signing, Lighting, Guarding: High-visibility clothing, helmet, gloves, footwear

Excavation Safety

Trench Collapse Prevention: Trench support equipment

Protection from Traffic Risks: Traffic control methods

Excavation Safety Zoning: 2m clearance on 30mph road

Excavation Safety Inspection: Start of each shift and after events

Site Specific Risk Assessment Importance: Identify risks and control measures

Backfill Materials and Reinstatement

Classification of Backfill Materials: Class A - D indicating suitability for reuse

Segregation of Materials: To prevent contamination and loss of fines

Protection from Drying: Keep covered at all times

Stockpiling of Unsuitable Materials: Seperate for removal to recycling site

Requirement for Reinstatement Compatibility: Compliance with specifications
